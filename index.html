Asado in Patagonia

After living in Chilean Patagonia for 12 months, I can say that the Asado is the best dish Chile has.

The Patagonia area spans across southern Chile and Argentina, and it is a place full of life and nature. It is like a place that man has not reached because you can see free-roaming animals like pumas huem√∫l and others go through the city as if nothing happened.

During this trip to Patagonia, I did not know what a real Asado was until I ate Asado with native people. It probably was the best Asado I ever had. To cook the Asado, they used Antarctic Beech wood, water, salt, garlic clove, and pebre for 3 hours. Three hours might sound like a lot of time, but it is a time I am willing to wait again.

Alaska's Salmons

I love Alaska; you always feel like you are part of nature and that you do not need to worry about anything at all, with one exception, food.

For some reason, when I am in Alaska, I always feel the need to catch/hunt my food, and this travel was not the exception. I was capable of fishing some of Alaska's well-known salmons, which some locals helped me to cook using their unique recipes.

I would not say that I am a lover of salmon, but holy molly, I would be lying if I said that I did not enjoy that salmon. It was so tender and full of flavors that I fill like I was entering a spiritual trance, so I had to slow down and eat little bites.

It is definitely a journey that I will do again.
